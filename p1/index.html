Relevant Range

\(\text{reg} S(1,b;d)=1\)

\(\text{pd} S(1,b;d)=\binom{d+1}{1}-1\)

The relevant range is the values of \(p\) for which the Betti numbers \(\beta_{p,q}(1,b;d)\) are not determined solely by the multigraded Hilbert series of \(S(1,b;d)\). This is the range in which we must compute the multigraded Betti numbers by computing the ranks of the accompanying differentials.

Verifications

Here we record ways in which our computations have been checked/verified:

  • For all \(d\geq0\) and all \(b< d\) the minimal resolution of \(S(1,b;d)\) is known to be given by an Eagon-Northcott complex. (For example, see Corollary 6.2 in [5] for a precise statement when \(b=0\).) From this it is possible to recover the graded, multigraded, and Schur graded Betti numbers. Our computations agree with these.
