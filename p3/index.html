Relevant Range

\(\text{reg} S(3,b;d)=\)

\(\text{pd} S(3,b;d)=\)

The relevant range is the values of \((p,q)\) for which the Betti numbers \(\beta_{p,q}(3,b;d)\) are non-zero and not determined solely by the multigraded Hilbert series of \(S(3,b;d)\). This is the range in which we must compute the multigraded Betti numbers by computing the ranks of the accompanying differentials.

For \(\mathbb{P}^{3}\) the relevant range can alternatively be characterized as the set of \((p,q)\) where \(K_{p,q}(3,b;d)\neq0\) and \(K_{p-1,q+1}(3,b;d)\neq0\), \(K_{p+1,q-1}(3,b;d)\neq0\),\(K_{p+2,q-2}(3,b;d)\neq0\), or \(K_{p-2,q+2}(3,b;d)\neq0\). A mixture of various results (see Rmk) gives shows \([K_{p,q}(3,b;d)\neq0\) for all \(p\) in the following range: \[ \binom{m+d}{d}-\binom{m+d-r-1}{m}-m\leq p \leq \binom{3+d}{2}+\binom{3-m+r}{3-m}-\binom{3-m+d}{3-m}-m-1 \] where \(m\) and \(r\) are the quotient and remainder of \(qd+b\) divided by \(d-1\). Unlike in the case when \(n=2\) these bounds are not known to be sharp, although they are conjectured to be.
