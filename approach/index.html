--- layout: default title: Approach description: SyzygyData, a project about computing syzygies. ---

We approach the problem of computing syzygies of \(\mathbb{P}^n\) via numerical linear algebra and high throughput/performance computing. In particular, we compute the cohomology of the Koszul complex. Continuing the notation from Background page \(K_{p,q}(n,b;d)\) may be computed as the cohomology of the complex: \[\bigwedge^{p+1}S_{d}\otimes S_{b+(q-1)d}\xrightarrow{\quad \partial_{p+1} \quad}\bigwedge^{p}S_{d}\otimes S_{b+qd}\xrightarrow{\quad \partial_{p} \quad}\bigwedge^{p-1}S_{d}\otimes S_{b+(q+1)d},\] where the differentials are given by \[\partial_{p}(m_1\wedge m_2\wedge \cdots \wedge m_p\otimes f)=\sum_{k=1}^p(-1)^km_1\wedge m_2\wedge \cdots \wedge \hat{m}_{k}\wedge \cdots \wedge m_p\otimes(m_kf).\] Note these differentials respect the \(\mathbb{Z}^{n+1}\)-grading on \(S\), and so the above complex may be decomposed into its multigraded stands, and so we may compute \(K_{p,q}(n,b;d)_{\textbf{a}}\) via the cohomology of the following subcomplex: \[\left(\bigwedge^{p+1}S_{d}\otimes S_{b+(q-1)d}\right)_{\textbf{a}}\xrightarrow{\quad \partial_{p+1,\textbf{a}} \quad}\left(\bigwedge^{p}S_{d}\otimes S_{b+qd}\right)_{\textbf{a}}\xrightarrow{\quad \partial_{p,\textbf{a}} \quad}\left(\bigwedge^{p-1}S_{d}\otimes S_{b+(q+1)d}\right)_{\textbf{a}}.\] Thus, computing the multigraded Betti numbers \(\beta_{p,\textbf{a}}(n,b;d)\) reduces to the linear algebra problem of computing the ranks of \(\partial_{p+1,\textbf{a}}\) and \(\partial_{p+1,\textbf{a}}\). With this in mind our computation can roughly be broken down into three steps.

Pre-Computation

We use known vanishing results and facts about Hilbert series to reduce the number of matrices whose rank we need to compute. We also use standard duality results to focus on simpler matrices in some cases.

Main Computation

We construct the remaining relevant matrices, and use an LU-decomposition algorithm and distributed, high throughput computations to compute the ranks of those matrices.

Post-Processing

We assemble our data to produce the total the multigraded Betti numbers and the standard Betti numbers, and we apply a highest weight decompo- sition algorithm to obtain the Schur module decompositions.
