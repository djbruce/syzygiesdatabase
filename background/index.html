Fix \(n>0\) and let \(S=\bf{k}[x_0,\ldots,x_n]\) be the standard graded polynomial ring. We denote the \(k\)th graded piece of \(S\) by \(S_k\). Now for any \(b\in \mathbb{Z}\) and \(d\in\mathbb{N}\) we let \[ S(b;d)\coloneqq \bigoplus_{k\in \mathbb{Z}}S_{dk+b}\subset, S\] which we think of as an \(R=\Sym(S_d)\)-module. Notice that \(S(b;d)\) corresponds to the pushforward of the sheaf \(\mathcal{O}_{\mathbb{P}^2}(b)\) by the \(d\)'uple embedding of \(\mathbb{P}^n\).
